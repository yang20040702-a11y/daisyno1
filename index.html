This project explores storytelling as a collaborative and evolving process between human and system. The website, *Co-writing Story Engine*, is designed as a simple interactive tool where a user contributes a fragment of a narrative, and the system responds by continuing the story. Rather than functioning as a conventional writing aid, the tool emphasizes unpredictability, rhythm, and shared authorship. Each exchange produces a story that neither side could fully control, creating a space where narrative emerges through dialogue. The structure of the interface reinforces this idea. The split screen separates input and output, yet the continuous flow of text on the right side visually merges both voices into a single narrative stream. This reflects the central concept of the project: boundaries between authorship are present, but fluid. The user remains an individual voice, while the system responds as a parallel presence, echoing the idea of coexistence explored in the stories themselves. The stories generated through this process are not final outcomes, but seeds. They serve as the initial conceptual material for a series of illustrations. Each narrative, shaped by spontaneity and layered perspectives, provides imagery, atmosphere, and emotional direction for visual interpretation. By translating these collaboratively written stories into illustrations, I extend the dialogue across mediums—from text to image. Through this project, I am interested in how creative control can be shared without being lost, and how unpredictability can become a productive force. The website becomes both a tool and a conceptual framework: a space where stories grow, shift, and generate new forms, ultimately bridging writing and visual art.
